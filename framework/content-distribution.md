# Content Distribution

Publishing is not distribution. The map and the calendar decide *what* to write; this
decides how each piece gets *seen* before organic traffic compounds. New content on a new
domain has no audience on day one — SEO is a 6–12 month engine, so distribution is what
produces signal (and early links, and AI citations) in the meantime.

This is the trimmed content-launch slice of a launch strategy, not a full GTM plan. It
inherits the suite's discipline: channels are matched to the brand's *actual* audience and
personas (from `entity-profile.json`), not a generic checklist, and **no reach or
engagement number is invented** — there is nothing to measure yet. Specific communities
and newsletters are discovered (`measured`, dated, via web_search); the plan proposes
channel *types* and the repurposing atoms.

## One piece → many atoms

A published article is raw material. Each becomes native content for a channel — same
substance, reshaped, never a bare "check out our post" link:

- **X/Twitter thread** from the question-H2 answers (they're already extractive).
- **LinkedIn founder POV** — the single insight, in first person.
- **Reddit/Quora answer** to a real question, linking back only where it genuinely helps.
- **Short-form video** — for a product that can demo itself, *dogfood the tool*: generate the ad
  about the topic. The distribution channel and the product demo are the same asset.
- **Newsletter section.**
- **Carousel / infographic** from a list or comparison table in the piece.

## Channel fit by node type

| Node shape | Lean into |
|---|---|
| Commercial core (tools, alternatives) | Retarget warm audiences; get into ranking roundups (hand to `link-opportunities`); comparison communities |
| Informational core (how-to, guides) | Community Q&A (Reddit/Quora), newsletter, short-form video |
| Outer / pillars (authority) | Syndication (Medium, LinkedIn article), thought-leadership social, aggregators *only if genuinely novel* |

Match to the personas, not the platform du jour: a solo Shopify founder, a lean
performance marketer, and an agency owner do not read the same places — the plan names the
audience so discovery targets *their* communities.

## Cadence

- **Published node:** Day 0 owned + community, Day 3–7 syndication, then an evergreen
  re-share roughly monthly. Distribution is repeated, not one-shot.
- **Planned node:** queue the atoms now; fire them on the node's publish week from
  `calendar.md`.

## The honest limit

This plans channels, atoms, and timing. It does not post, and it cannot manufacture an
audience you don't have — early distribution is manual founder/team work (or an agent that
actually posts). Its job is to make sure nothing ships into silence.

## Feed the loop
- Community questions you answer → candidate query-network additions (`topical-map-builder`).
- A piece that earns a link or citation while promoting → record it (feeds
  `link-opportunities` / `answer-engine-optimizer`).
- Once traffic exists, `seo-performance-tracker` (GSC) tells you which distributed pieces
  actually converted attention into rankings — closing the loop back to measurement.
